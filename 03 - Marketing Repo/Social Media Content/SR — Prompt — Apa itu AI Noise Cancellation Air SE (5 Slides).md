### SERIES OVERVIEW

**Topic:** What is AI Noise Cancellation — and how it works on the Saramonic Air SE
**Content arc:** Educate first (Slides 1–3), then land the product angle (Slide 4), close with brand (Slide 5).
**Tone:** Clear, confident, technically grounded — not a Wikipedia explainer, not a sales pitch. Write like someone who actually uses pro audio gear.
**Visual language:** Clean, concept-driven. Abstract/graphic-led Slides 1–3 to illustrate the concept. Product-focused on Slide 4. Brand-only on Slide 5.

---

### SLIDE 1 — COVER / HOOK

**Goal:** Spark curiosity. Make someone who skims want to read Slide 2.

**Layout:**
- Top: Series label tag — small, uppercase — `TECH EXPLAINED`
- Center: Bold concept visual — e.g. sound wave graphic split between noise and clean signal, or a visual contrast of chaotic vs. clean audio
- Bottom: Headline text block

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Apa itu AI Noise Cancellation?`
- Subhead: `Bukan sekadar filter biasa.`

**Visual notes:**
- No product on this slide — concept only
- Visual should communicate "noise vs. clarity" without being too literal
- Saramonic logo bottom-right, small

---

### SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM (What noise sounds like)

**Goal:** Make the audience feel the problem before you explain the solution.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `01` or `THE PROBLEM`
- Center: Graphic illustrating background noise sources — crowd, traffic, HVAC, wind — represented as abstract noise interference on a waveform or signal
- Bottom: Explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Noise itu musuh audio.`
- Body: `Background noise — angin, keramaian, AC — merusak rekaman yang seharusnya bersih.`

**Visual notes:**
- Graphic tone: slightly chaotic to represent noise — but still on-brand and controlled
- Use visual metaphor, not literal photos of crowds or streets
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 3 — THE SOLUTION (How AI Noise Cancellation works)

**Goal:** Explain the technology simply. One clear idea, one clear visual.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `02` or `THE FIX`
- Center: Diagram or graphic showing AI processing — e.g. dirty signal in → AI filter → clean signal out. Keep it simple: 3-part flow visual.
- Bottom: Explanation text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `AI belajar bedain suara vs. noise.`
- Body: `AI Noise Cancellation deteksi dan isolasi suara yang kamu mau — buang sisanya secara real-time.`

**Visual notes:**
- Flow diagram: simple, 3-step — noisy input → AI processing → clean output
- Use iconography or abstract signal graphic — no photos
- Saramonic logo bottom-right

---

### SLIDE 4 — PRODUCT ANCHOR (Air SE + AI NC feature)

**Goal:** Connect the concept to the product. Make it concrete and desirable.

**Layout:**
- Top-left: Slide label — `03` or `ON THE AIR SE`
- Center: Product shot of Saramonic Air SE — prominent, clean background
- Bottom: Feature call-out text

**On-image copy:**
- Headline: `Saramonic Air SE punya itu.`
- Body: Extract 1–2 specific AI NC specs or use cases from the uploaded manual — e.g. how many dB of noise reduction, which modes, what environments it's optimized for. Keep to max 2 lines.

> **Note to Claude Design:** Pull the specific AI Noise Cancellation spec/feature detail from the uploaded product manual. Do not fabricate numbers. If spec is not in the manual, use a use-case statement instead — e.g. "Dioptimalkan untuk rekaman outdoor, interview, dan vlog handheld."

**Visual notes:**
- This is the only slide with product photography — give it visual weight
- Clean, high-contrast product shot — white or dark brand background
- Saramonic logo bottom-right, same position as previous slides

---

### SLIDE 5 — CLOSING / WATERMARK

**Goal:** Brand close. No product info, no CTA, no explanation.

**Layout:**

- Center: Saramonic logo (large, light/inverted version)

**Visual notes:**

- Minimal elements: logo + tagline only
- High contrast: light on dark (or reverse per design system)
